The Cricket World Cup 2023 was the 13th edition of the global cricket tournament that took place in India from 5 October to 19 November 20231 Australia won the tournament for the sixth time, defeating India by six wickets in the final at Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad23 Virat Kohli was the player of the tournament and also the leading run-scorer with 765 runs in 11 matches. Mohammed Shami was the highest wicket-taker with 24 wickets in seven matches2

The tournament featured 10 teams, who played each other in a round-robin format. The top four teams advanced to the semi-finals, where India beat New Zealand and Australia beat South Africa2 The tournament witnessed many records being broken, such as the highest individual score (201* by Glenn Maxwell), the most sixes in an innings (18 by Shreyas Iyer), the most wickets in a match (7 by Dilshan Madushanka), and the most spectators in a World Cup (1,250,307)3

The tournament also had some controversies, such as the boycott threat by Pakistan over the hosting issue, the penalties for slow over-rates, and the umpiring errors3 However, the tournament was widely praised for its high-quality cricket, thrilling matches, and passionate fans45
